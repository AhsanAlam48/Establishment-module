विद्या परमं बलम - KNOWLEDGE IS THE SUPREME STRENGTH.

National Institute of Technology, Jamshedpur was initially founded as Regional Institute of Technology in 1960. It was among the first eight Regional Engineering Colleges (RECs) established as part of the Second Five-Year Plan. It was the only REC which was set-up as an institute of technology rather than a college of engineering at that time. NIT Jamshedpur, since its establishment in 1960, was in Singhbhum district. After division of the district in 1990, its position came under the West Singhbhum district. After further division of the West Singhbhum district in 2001, NIT Jamshedpur finally came under Saraikela-Kharswan district of Jharkhand. The college was upgraded to National Institute of Technology on 27 December 2002, and granted the status of a Deemed University. The college has intake of 3000 Undergraduates & about 200 graduates students.
